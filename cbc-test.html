Understanding Complete Blood Count (CBC) Test Report

Published: July 2026 | Written by Lab Experts

A Complete Blood Count (CBC) is one of the most common blood tests. It helps doctors check your overall health and detect a wide range of conditions, including anemia, infection, and leukemia.

1. Red Blood Cells (RBC) & Hemoglobin

Red blood cells carry oxygen from your lungs to the rest of your body. Hemoglobin is the protein in RBCs that holds the oxygen.

  • Low Levels: May indicate Anemia, causing fatigue, weakness, or pale skin.
  • High Levels: Can be caused by dehydration, smoking, or living at a high altitude.

2. White Blood Cells (WBC)

White blood cells are part of your immune system. They help your body fight infections and diseases.

  • High WBC Count: Usually means your body is fighting an infection or inflammation.
  • Low WBC Count: Can put you at a higher risk of catching infections, often caused by bone marrow issues or certain medications.

3. Platelets (PLT)

Platelets help your blood clot to stop bleeding when you get a cut or injury.

  • Low Platelet Count (Thrombocytopenia): Can cause easy bruising or prolonged bleeding. Common in viral infections like Dengue.
  • High Platelet Count: May cause abnormal blood clotting risks.

Standard Reference Ranges

Parameter Normal Range (Adults) Primary Function
Hemoglobin (Hb) 12.0 - 17.5 g/dL Oxygen Transport
WBC Count 4,000 - 11,000 /µL Immune Defense
Platelet Count 150,000 - 450,000 /µL Blood Clotting

Disclaimer: Reference ranges can vary slightly between different laboratories. Always consult with a qualified medical practitioner or doctor to get a final evaluation of your report.

Lipid Profile Test (کولیسٹرول ٹیسٹ)

Comprehensive evaluation of cardiovascular health, cholesterol fractions, and triglycerides.

What is a Lipid Profile?

A lipid profile is a panel of blood tests used to find abnormalities in lipids (fats and cholesterol). The test measures total cholesterol, HDL (good cholesterol), LDL (bad cholesterol), and triglycerides to evaluate the risk of developing cardiovascular diseases.

Clinical Parameters & Reference Ranges

Parameter Optimal / Normal Range Borderline / High Clinical Significance
Total Cholesterol < 200 mg/dL ≥ 240 mg/dL Overall amount of cholesterol in blood.
LDL (Bad Cholesterol) < 100 mg/dL ≥ 160 mg/dL High levels lead to plaque buildup in arteries.
HDL (Good Cholesterol) > 40 mg/dL (Men)
> 50 mg/dL (Women)		 < 40 mg/dL (Low Risk) Helps remove bad cholesterol from the bloodstream.
Triglycerides < 150 mg/dL ≥ 200 mg/dL Type of fat used for energy; high levels risk pancreas/heart.

Patient Preparation (Pre-test Requirements)

  • Fasting: Strict 9 to 12 hours of fasting is highly recommended before sample collection. Only plain water is allowed.
  • Diet & Alcohol: Avoid heavy fatty meals and alcohol 24 hours prior to the test.

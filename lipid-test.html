Lipid Profile Test

A comprehensive blood panel evaluated to assess cardiovascular health, circulating cholesterol fractions, and total triglyceride levels.

Clinical Parameters & Reference Ranges

Test Parameter Optimal / Normal Range Borderline / High Risk Clinical Significance
Total Cholesterol Less than 200 mg/dL 240 mg/dL or Higher Measures the overall amount of cholesterol in the blood.
LDL Cholesterol Less than 100 mg/dL 160 mg/dL or Higher Often called 'Bad Cholesterol'; high levels lead to arterial plaque buildup.
Non-HDL Cholesterol Less than 130 mg/dL 160 mg/dL or Higher Calculated as Total Cholesterol minus HDL. Represents all atherogenic particles.
HDL Cholesterol More than 40 - 50 mg/dL Less than 40 mg/dL (Low) Known as 'Good Cholesterol'; helps clear atherogenic lipids from circulation.
Triglycerides Less than 150 mg/dL 200 mg/dL or Higher A major type of fat used for energy storage; elevated levels increase risk of pancreatic issues.

Patient Preparation & Pre-test Requirements

  • Fasting Guidelines:
    A strict 9 to 12 hours of fasting is required prior to sample collection. Only plain, unflavored water is permitted during this period.
  • Dietary Restrictions:
    Avoid consuming heavily fatty meals, high-sugar items, or alcohol for at least 24 hours before the test to prevent transient spikes in lipid markers.

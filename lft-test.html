Liver Function Tests (LFT): A Clinical Overview

Updated: July 2026 | Verified by Laboratory Professionals
What is an LFT Panel? Liver Function Tests (LFTs) are groups of blood tests designed to evaluate the clinical status of the liver and biliary tract. They measure specific enzymes, proteins, and substances synthesized or cleared by the liver to screen for hepatic injury, infections, and metabolic abnormalities.

1. Clinical Significance of Liver Enzymes

Enzymes are intracellular proteins released into the bloodstream in large quantities during hepatocyte injury or biliary obstruction:

Aminotransferases (ALT & AST)

  • Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT / SGPT): Primarily localized to the liver. Elevated levels are highly specific for hepatic parenchymal injury, such as acute viral hepatitis or fatty liver disease.
  • Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST / SGOT): Found in liver tissue, heart, and skeletal muscles. An AST higher than ALT often indicates alcoholic liver disease or advanced cirrhosis.

Cholestatic Enzymes (ALP & GGT)

  • Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP): Associated with the biliary epithelial cells and bone. Marked elevations usually point to biliary tract obstruction (e.g., gallstones) or bone disease.
  • Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT): Highly sensitive marker for biliary tract stress and alcohol ingestion; used to confirm if ALP elevation is of hepatic origin.

2. Bilirubin Metabolism & Cleansing

Bilirubin is a byproduct of red blood cell breakdown that must be conjugated by the liver for excretion:

  • Total Bilirubin: The combined total of both processed and unprocessed pigments. High levels manifest clinically as Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes).
  • Direct (Conjugated) Bilirubin: Water-soluble form processed by the liver. Elevation suggests post-hepatic obstruction or biliary drainage problems.
  • Indirect (Unconjugated) Bilirubin: Pre-hepatic form. Elevation typically indicates accelerated RBC destruction (Hemolytic Anemia) or conjugation failure.

3. Synthetic Function Markers

The liver synthesizes critical proteins essential for maintaining osmotic pressure and immunity:

  • Albumin: The major blood protein made by the liver. Low levels reflect chronic liver damage (decreased manufacturing capacity) or severe malnutrition.
  • Globulin & A/G Ratio: Measures total immune proteins. Alterations help diagnose chronic inflammatory states, autoimmune hepatitis, or multiple myeloma.

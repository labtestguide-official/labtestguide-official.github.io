Liver Function Tests (LFT): A Clinical Overview

A comprehensive panel used to screen for, diagnose, and monitor liver disease or damage by measuring specific enzymes, proteins, and waste products in the blood.

Clinical Parameters & Reference Ranges (Adults)

Test Parameter Optimal / Normal Range Borderline / High Risk Core Medical Function / Focus
SGPT / ALT 7 to 56 U/L Above 56 U/L Specific liver cell damage marker; tracks inflammation.
SGOT / AST 10 to 40 U/L Above 40 U/L Enzyme found in liver, heart, and muscles; tracks tissue injury.
Total Bilirubin 0.1 to 1.2 mg/dL Above 1.2 mg/dL Biliary secretion & clearance tracking; Jaundice assessment.
ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase) 44 to 147 U/L Above 147 U/L Bile duct blockage and bone metabolism indicator.
Total Protein 6.0 to 8.3 g/dL Outside Normal Range Overall systemic and nutritional status evaluator.
Serum Albumin 3.4 to 5.4 g/dL Less than 3.4 g/dL (Low) Tracks hepatic synthetic balance and fluid retention.

Clinical Insights & Liver Health Management

Detailed analysis of major tracking indicators within a complete LFT panel:

Biliary Secretion & Clearance Waste Processing
Clinical Significance (Bilirubin):

Bilirubin is generated through the normal breakdown of red blood cells. The liver filters it from the blood and eliminates it through bile. High circulating levels trigger Jaundice (yellowing of eyes and skin), which frequently points to structural blockages in the bile ducts, gallstones, liver injury, or excess RBC destruction (Hemolysis).

How it is Managed:

Requires evaluating the clear physiological root cause. Proper hydration aids basic toxic clearance. Obstructions like gallstones require direct surgical or endoscopic clearance, while virus-induced issues require dedicated medical care.

Cellular Injury Indicators Enzyme Tracking
Clinical Significance (SGPT & SGOT):

When liver cells are stressed or injured, intracellular enzymes spill directly into the bloodstream. SGPT (ALT) is highly specific to liver health, and spikes point to conditions like fatty liver, viral hepatitis, or drug-induced damage. SGOT (AST) is present in the liver but also in skeletal muscles and the heart; concurrent elevations in both parameters strongly confirm standard liver cellular injury.

How it is Managed:

Controlled efficiently by removing metabolic burdens—restricting alcohol, adjusting high-fat diets, lowering body mass index (BMI), and eliminating hepatotoxic over-the-counter painkillers or unprescribed supplements.

Synthetic Capacity Markers Proteins
Clinical Significance (Albumin & Total Protein):

The liver is the body's primary manufacturing hub for vital blood proteins. Albumin maintains oncotic pressure, preventing fluid from leaking out of blood vessels into tissues (which causes Edema/Ascites). Low protein levels or an abnormal Albumin-to-Globulin (A/G) ratio signal chronic liver conditions like Cirrhosis, where the organ loses its long-term synthetic capacity.

How it is Managed:

Chronic deficits are addressed through targeted dietary adjustments, supporting raw nutrient assimilation, managing fluid balance under expert guidance, and continuously protecting the remaining functional liver tissue.

Patient Preparation & Pre-test Requirements

  • Fasting Guidelines:
    An overnight fast of 8 to 12 hours is highly recommended for accurate baseline values. Plain water is permitted.
  • Pre-test Restrictions:
    Avoid heavy physical exertion, alcohol consumption, and non-essential clinical drugs for 24 hours to prevent artificial enzyme fluctuations.

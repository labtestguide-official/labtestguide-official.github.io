Renal Function Profile (RFT / KFT)

Comprehensive diagnostic assessment evaluating glomerular filtration, nitrogenous waste clearance, and electrolyte-metabolic renal equilibrium.

Core Clinical Parameters

1. Serum Creatinine

Adult Male Range: 0.6 - 1.2 mg/dL
Adult Female Range: 0.5 - 1.1 mg/dL
Specimen: Serum (Yellow Top)

Clinical Significance: Creatinine is a steady byproduct of muscle metabolism (creatine breakdown). Since healthy kidneys filter it entirely without reabsorption, it serves as the most reliable direct marker for overall renal clearance capacity.

Causes of Elevated Levels:
  • Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) or Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
  • Renal artery stenosis or severe dehydration (reduced perfusion)
  • Urinary tract obstruction (calculi, prostatic hypertrophy)

2. Blood Urea

Normal Range: 15 - 45 mg/dL
Critical Value: > 100 mg/dL
Specimen: Serum (Yellow Top)

Clinical Significance: Urea is the primary nitrogenous waste product formed in the liver during protein catabolism via the urea cycle. It is filtered by the glomeruli and partially reabsorbed in the tubules, making it highly sensitive to both renal and extra-renal metabolic factors.

Causes of Elevated Levels (Azotemia):
  • Prerenal factors: Severe dehydration, shock, gastrointestinal hemorrhage, or high protein intake
  • Renal factors: Glomerulonephritis, tubular necrosis, or acute renal failure

3. Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN)

Normal Range: 7 - 20 mg/dL
BUN/Creatinine Ratio: 10:1 to 20:1
Specimen: Serum / Plasma

Clinical Significance: BUN specifically quantifies the nitrogen portion of urea in the bloodstream. Evaluating the BUN-to-Creatinine ratio is a crucial diagnostic step to differentiate whether renal impairment is caused by intrinsic kidney tissue damage or dehydration.

4. eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate)

Normal Level: > 90 mL/min/1.73 m²
CKD Threshold: < 60 mL/min/1.73 m²
Calculation Type: CKD-EPI / MDRD Equation

Clinical Significance: eGFR is mathematically calculated utilizing serum creatinine levels, age, gender, and race. It represents the actual volume of fluid filtered by the kidney’s glomeruli per unit of time, serving as the gold standard for staging Chronic Kidney Disease (Stages 1 through 5).

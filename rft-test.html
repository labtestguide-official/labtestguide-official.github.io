Renal Function Tests (RFT): A Clinical Overview

Updated: July 2026 | Verified by Laboratory Professionals
What is an RFT Panel? Renal Function Tests (RFTs) are a group of vital investigations performed to evaluate how efficiently the kidneys are filtering metabolic waste and functioning overall.

Clinical Significance of Key Parameters

The kidneys act as filtration units. When their function decreases, these specific waste products begin to build up in the bloodstream:

1. Serum Creatinine & eGFR

  • Serum Creatinine: A waste product from normal muscle breakdown. Elevated levels directly point to reduced kidney filtering capacity.
  • eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate): Calculated using your creatinine value. It is the international standard used to determine the exact stage of kidney health.

2. Serum Urea & BUN

  • Serum Urea: Formed during protein metabolism in the liver. High levels indicate that the kidneys are unable to clear protein waste effectively, or can indicate severe dehydration.
  • BUN (Blood Urea Nitrogen): Measures the nitrogen portion of urea in the blood. Evaluating the BUN-to-Creatinine ratio helps doctors understand whether kidney dysfunction is due to lack of fluids or actual tissue damage.

Standard Clinical Reference Ranges

Serum Creatinine Male: 0.7 - 1.3 mg/dL
Female: 0.6 - 1.1 mg/dL		 Most stable marker for kidney clearance

Important Medical Disclaimer: Lab results must always be cross-referenced with your doctor. Temporary fluctuations can happen based on daily water intake, diet, and physical activity.
